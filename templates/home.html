The first distinctive title. It will blow your mind.

The human face is a complex and highly distinctive part of the body. In fact, it is one of the most complex signaling systems available to us; it includes more than 40 structurally and functionally independent muscles, each of which can be operated independently of the others; and it is one of the most powerful indicators of emotion.

Oh yeah, that's good. See for yourself.

When we laugh or cry, we display our emotions, allowing others to peek into our minds as they “read” our faces based on changes in major facial components, such as the eyes, eyebrows, eyelids, nose, and lips.

Finally, this. Checkmate.

All the muscles in our body are supported by nerves that connect from all over the body to the spinal cord and brain. This nerve communication is bidirectional, meaning that the nerve causes muscle contractions based on signals from the brain, and at the same time sends information about the condition of the muscle to the brain.
